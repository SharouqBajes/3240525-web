Petra

Petra is Jordan's most famous archaeological site, also called the Rose City. UNESCO listed it as a World Heritage Site.

Facts:

  • Located in southern Jordan
  • Best visiting season: Spring and Autumn
  • Entrance fee: $70
Feature Detail
Main Attraction The Treasury
Built 5th century BC
Visitors per year More than 1 million

Wadi Rum

Wadi Rum is known as the Valley of the Moon, a protected desert area with giant sandstone mountains.

Activities:

  1. Jeep Tours
  2. Star Camping
  3. Hiking & Rock Climbing
  4. Hot Air Balloon Rides

Dead Sea

The Dead Sea is the lowest point on Earth, famous for its salt and mineral mud.

  • Float easily due to high salt content
  • Health resorts and spa
  • Open year-round

Jerash

Jerash is famous for its well-preserved Roman ruins including theaters, temples, and streets with columns.

Highlights:

  1. Oval Forum
  2. Hadrian's Arch
  3. Hippodrome
  4. South Theater
